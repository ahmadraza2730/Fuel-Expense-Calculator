06 — REFERENCE

General mileage tips

Contextual tips appear automatically after you calculate your average. These are the standing rules worth knowing regardless.

◐

Tire Pressure

Under-inflated tires are the single most common mileage killer. Check pressure monthly, cold, against the sticker inside your door frame — not the tire's max rating.

◑

Driving Style

Hard acceleration and late braking cost more fuel than almost any mechanical issue. Anticipate traffic, roll off the throttle early, and keep RPMs in the efficient mid-range.

◒

Idling & AC

Long idling at signals burns fuel for zero distance. AC on the highway costs less than open windows at speed — but AC at idle adds up fast in city traffic.

◓

Weight & Load

Every extra 50kg you carry costs measurable mileage, especially on hatchbacks and bikes. Clear out the boot of anything you don't need for the trip.

◔

Servicing

A clogged air filter, worn spark plugs, or old engine oil can quietly cost you 10–15% average. Stick to your service interval even if the car "feels fine."

◕

Fuel Quality

Stick to filling stations you trust. Inconsistent octane or contaminated fuel shows up as an average that swings for no clear reason.
